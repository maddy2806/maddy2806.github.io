## Conclusion

The analysis suggests that optional pre-lecture videos could create value for students. If many students rated pre-lecture videos highly, this supports the idea that students want extra preparation materials. However, the survey data does not prove that videos would improve grades, so future data should track whether students actually watch the videos and whether their performance improves. One trade-off is that videos would take time for instructional staff to create. To reduce that cost, the videos should be short, optional, and focused only on the most difficult topics.
